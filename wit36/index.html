§ 01The invitation00:00:00

A room. Three hours. You, alone.

No photographer, no witness. You bring what you want and do what you want. Whatever happens in that room is your doing, and it is the work.

A machine takes 36 frames at moments none of us can predict, yours included. You can’t choose them, reshoot them, or edit them.

The finished piece, all 36 frames, is your work, under your name. You’ll see it for the first time at the opening, at the same moment as everyone else, and you take it home. You answer for the whole three hours, not the best of them.

Our part is only the frame: the room, the time, the ban on choosing, the number 36.

Total control of the action.
Zero control of the output.

For three hours you direct everything and edit nothing. The work sits in that gap: your grip on the room against the machine’s indifference to what it catches.

§ 02The protocol00:00 → 03:00
  1. 00:00

    You enter alone for 180 minutes. Watches and phones stay at the door; bring anything else you like. The door closes.

  2. 36 exposures across the three hours. The count is announced; the moments aren’t. The schedule is sealed in an envelope you sign, and never opened.

  3. No choosing. No reshooting. No editing, by you or the studio. All 36 are printed and shown in full, in the order taken, at one size.

  4. Cover the light and the frames come out black, and print black. A black frame is an act, not a fault.

  5. The camera works alone. No one sees a frame before it is printed: the files run a sealed pipeline straight to the press.

  6. 03:00

    The door opens; you step out. You’ll see your 36 frames for the first time at the opening, alongside everyone else.
