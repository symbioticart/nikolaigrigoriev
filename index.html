Nikolai Grigoriev
syncing the present body…

Variations 87 is a symbiotic artwork — a genre originated by Nikolai Grigoriev, in which a painting is not composed by the artist but grown from a single living body. The body is both the subject and the instrument.

The painting is generated from my own physiological data, recorded continuously by a wearable biometric sensor (Oura Ring). It is not a fixed picture but a living loop: each night the body writes the next day, the work syncs it the following morning, and the canvas opens on the most recent self. The image you see is only the current frame of an ongoing relationship.

Fourteen physiological measurements are read from each 24-hour cycle, normalized against my own history, and mapped to independent visual parameters. The same body-state always produces the same painting — the process is deterministic; the seed is the body, not the date.

Silence

The loop can break. When the signal stops — the ring removed, the body still — the work does not pretend. The last day freezes, its colour drains, and it dissolves toward nothing in proportion to the silence. A gap is not smoothed over; it is the most honest image the work can hold. The painting is built to register an ending, not to outlive one.

Three Registers

The body never speaks in one voice. The foundation — broad colour grounds — carries the recovered, parasympathetic body: deep sleep and heart-rate variability. The modulation — curved middle gestures — is the waking self: readiness and activity. The tremor — a swarm of small, beaded marks — is the agitated surface: restlessness, breath, an elevated pulse. The width of variation across all of them is heart-rate variability itself: the variability of the heart becomes the variability of the hand.

Provenance

The work exists as a single continuous edition — edition of 1, continuous. What is owned is not a file but the loop itself: the right to a lifelong co-evolution with the body, and the work's living connection to it. Every day's state is deterministic and reproducible from the body that made it; that reproducibility is its certificate of authenticity. The image on screen is a frame; the work is the relationship.
