Symbiotic Art

Symbiotic art is a generative practice in which the artist's own body becomes both subject and instrument. Physiological data — captured involuntarily by wearable sensors during sleep and waking — drives every visual decision in the painting. The body narrates itself in the grammar of oil on canvas.

No composition is designed. No color is chosen. Fourteen biometric measurements from each 24-hour cycle are normalized and mapped to independent visual parameters: stroke density, palette probability, curvature, weight, spread, direction. The same data always produces the same painting. The process is deterministic — the art is the body's unconscious signature.

Method

Each work in this collection interprets the same biometric dataset through a different artistic lens — a different set of rules governing how physiology becomes paint. The lenses are named after painters whose formal vocabularies inform the translation: Rothko's color fields, De Keyser's sparse marks, Von Heyl's competing systems, Bess's hypnagogic symbols, Shiraga's physical density.

The paintings are rendered through a custom oil brush simulation (p5.oil.js) that models bristle physics, paint loading, and surface texture in WebGL2. The result is not illustration of data — it is data made physical, the body's own rhythms given form through the accumulated weight of simulated pigment.

Source

All data is captured by Oura Ring Generation 3, worn continuously. The dataset spans 97 consecutive days from January to June 2025. Each day yields: readiness score, sleep score, heart rate variability, resting heart rate, deep and REM sleep percentage, sleep efficiency, onset latency, restless periods, body temperature deviation, workout intensity, and respiratory rate.

Artist

Nikolai Grigoriev. The work exists at the intersection of generative art, data visualization, and painting — but belongs fully to none of these. It is a new form: the body as author, the algorithm as medium, the painting as portrait.
